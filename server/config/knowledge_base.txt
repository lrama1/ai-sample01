# Sample Knowledge Base

## Geography
- The capital of France is Paris.
- The Amazon River is the second longest river in the world, located in South America.
- Mount Everest, located in the Himalayas, is the highest mountain in the world.

## History
- The Declaration of Independence was signed on July 4, 1776.
- The Roman Empire was one of the largest empires in ancient history, lasting from 27 BC to AD 476.
- The Industrial Revolution began in the late 18th century and led to major changes in manufacturing and industry.

## Science
- Water is composed of two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom (H2O).
- The speed of light in a vacuum is approximately 299,792 kilometers per second.
- Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants use sunlight to synthesize foods from carbon dioxide and water.

## Technology
- The first programmable computer, the Z3, was created by Konrad Zuse in 1941.
- The internet is a global network of interconnected computers that communicate using standardized protocols.
- Artificial intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines that are programmed to think and learn.

## Literature
- "To Kill a Mockingbird" is a novel by Harper Lee, published in 1960.
- William Shakespeare is known for his plays such as "Hamlet," "Romeo and Juliet," and "Macbeth."
- "1984" is a dystopian novel by George Orwell, published in 1949.

## Miscellaneous
- The Great Wall of China is one of the most famous landmarks in the world, stretching over 13,000 miles.
- The Mona Lisa is a famous painting by Leonardo da Vinci, housed in the Louvre Museum in Paris.
- The Olympic Games are held every four years and feature athletes from around the world competing in various sports.

## Employee Benefits
- Deductibles are described in detail at the "Health Options" section of the Employee Handbook.
